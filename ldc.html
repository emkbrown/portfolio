Lunar Doula Collective

I am a member of Tech 4 Social Good, a student organization at the University of Michigan that focuses on discussing tech and social justice as well as developing skills to use in the tech industry for good rather than evil. I was selected to join Tech 4 Social Good's project team, which partnered with Lunar Doula Collective, a start up at the University of Michigan which provides support to women facing pregnancy loss or termination at no cost.

Our Task

In order to effectively support women facing pregnancy loss and termination, Lunar Doula Collective needs a website containing information about their services, their history, and a volunteer management system that can be easily used by volunteers and managed by administrators at Lunar Doula Collective.

Our Methodology

Our project team split into two subteams for front-end design and back-end development. As a UX Design student, I took the lead on the front-end design team. I work directly with the coordinators at Lunar Doula Collective to understand their vision for their website and what message and feeling they want the site to express to visitors. As students in computer science and UX Design, we looked to Professor Nikola Banovic for guidance on Human Centered Design. He guided us through a workshop about Human Centered Design to help us plan our meetings with Lunar Doula Collective, and create a workflow that meets our needs as a student team.
